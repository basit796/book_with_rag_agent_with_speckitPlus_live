## Prompt

Create a detailed feature specification for Module 1: Introduction to Physical AI for a book on humanoid robotics.

## Context
This is the first module of a multi-module book that will teach readers about Physical AI and humanoid robotics. The book follows a progression from concepts ‚Üí middleware (ROS 2) ‚Üí simulation (Isaac) ‚Üí AI-driven control.

## Feature Description

Module Number: 1
Module Title: Introduction to Physical AI

### 1. Module Purpose
- Establish the conceptual foundation of Physical AI and embodied intelligence
- Explain why robotics is fundamentally different from software-only AI
- Frame the motivation for humanoid robots in human environments
- Prepare the reader for ROS 2, simulation, and AI-driven control in later modules

### 2. Target Reader State
**Before:**
- Basic understanding of AI as software models (ML, LLMs)
- Familiarity with Python at a beginner level
- No robotics or hardware experience assumed

**After:**
- Reader can explain what Physical AI is
- Reader understands embodied intelligence vs digital intelligence
- Reader understands the role of sensors, actuators, and physical constraints
- Reader can articulate why simulation-first development is required

**Not Expected Yet:**
- Writing ROS 2 code
- Understanding kinematics or dynamics
- Using simulators or Isaac tools

### 3. Learning Outcomes
- Reader can define Physical AI and embodied intelligence
- Reader can explain why physical laws matter for AI systems
- Reader can identify core components of a robot (sensors, actuators, brain)
- Reader can describe why humanoid robots are useful in human spaces
- Reader can explain why robotics workflows differ from web/software workflows

### 4. Chapter Breakdown (High-Level)
- **Chapter 1: From Digital AI to Physical AI**  
  Concepts: Digital vs embodied intelligence  
  Depth: Conceptual

- **Chapter 2: The Physical World as a Constraint**  
  Concepts: Physics, uncertainty, noise, latency  
  Depth: Conceptual

- **Chapter 3: Anatomy of a Robot**  
  Concepts: Sensors, actuators, compute, feedback loops  
  Depth: Conceptual

- **Chapter 4: Why Humanoids?**  
  Concepts: Human-centered environments, form factor  
  Depth: Conceptual

### 5. Concepts & Mental Models
- Robot as a closed-loop control system
- Sense ‚Üí Think ‚Üí Act ‚Üí Sense loop
- AI model vs AI system
- Body as part of intelligence

### 6. Technical Scope
**Included:**
- High-level robot architecture
- Physical constraints and uncertainty
- Conceptual discussion of humanoids

**Excluded:**
- Math-heavy physics
- Control theory
- Any ROS or simulator usage

**Deferred:**
- Middleware (Module 2)
- Simulation (Module 3)

### 7. Examples & Demonstrations
- Thought experiment: LLM controlling a robot arm
- Comparison: Chatbot vs robot assistant
- Real-world robot failures due to ignored physics

### 8. Common Misconceptions
- "Robotics is just AI + motors"
- "LLMs can directly control robots safely"
- "Simulation is optional"

### 9. Success Validation
- Reader can verbally explain why robotics is harder than software AI
- Reader can explain embodied intelligence in simple terms

### 10. Constraints
- No equations
- No code
- No hardware purchasing assumptions
